My Favorite Movies-

Honestly, I don't have many reasons for this being one of my favorites beyond the fact that I enjoy musicals. there is a older version with Liam Neeson and such in it, but I prefer the more recent movie with Anne Hathaway.



Les Miserables -In 19th-century France, Jean Valjean, who for decades has been hunted by the ruthless policeman Javert after breaking parole, agrees to care for a factory worker's daughter. The decision changes their lives forever. (IMBD)

I like this movie because of its cast. I think they did a good job with the movie and it is pretty cute, a nice heartwarming story with a happy ending. Romance isn't my favorite genre but I just can't help but enjoy any movie with that guy from The Hangover, Katniss, and Robbie De Niro.

Silver Linings Playbook -After a stint in a mental institution, former teacher Pat Solitano moves back in with his parents and tries to reconcile with his ex-wife. Things get more challenging when Pat meets Tiffany, a mysterious girl with problems of her own.(IMBD)

Like most young men between the ages of 18 and 25 I like Quentin Tarintino movies and my favorite is Reservoir Dogs. This is one of the first Quentin Tarintino I remember watching with my older brother, we don't have much in common but we do have a similar taste in movies so this one left a good impression on me and is a nice reminder of family... also excessive violence and dudes in suits, whats not to love?

Reservoir Dogs -After a simple jewelry heist goes terribly wrong, the surviving criminals begin to suspect that one of them is a police informant.(IMBD)

Kiss of the Fur Queen-So my favorite book changes pretty frequently but my go to is Kiss of the Fur Queen by Tomson Highway. It was published in 1998 and was published again in after being translated to French in 2004. The story takes place in the '50s and focus's on two Cree brothers that are sent to a boarding school where they are abused by the priests there which scars them and shapes the boys into the young men they become as the novel progresses. I recommend this book mostly because everyone can interpret the story how they like and it brings light of the cruelty of assimilation practices used at these boarding schools that happened in North America.
